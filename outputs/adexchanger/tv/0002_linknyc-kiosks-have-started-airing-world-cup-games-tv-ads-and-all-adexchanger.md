## Summary

New York City’s LinkNYC digital kiosks have started livestreaming World Cup matches from Telemundo, including all TV ads, leveraging their capability to stream HLS feeds directly from broadcasters. This initiative began with broadcast rights secured for Knicks playoff games and expanded to World Cup games shown on about 200 of the 4,000 kiosks. Intersection, which manages LinkNYC’s content and marketing, enabled this seamless livestream integration. The move blends out-of-home (OOH) advertising with linear TV-style streaming, showing the potential of digital kiosks beyond traditional ad displays. The streaming events temporarily pre-empt normal ad inventory, emphasizing community benefit over immediate revenue. Measurement relies on third-party location and demographic data, with targeting based on geographic and contextual models rather than household-level data typical in CTV.

## Problem / Workflow Challenge

- Traditional OOH screens are limited to static or looping ads, restricting engagement possibilities.
- Livestreaming on public digital kiosks raises challenges in content rights management and advertising integration.
- Measurement and audience targeting in public spaces lack the precision of household or device-level CTV models.
- Monetization of screen inventory is disrupted during livestream events, creating trade-offs between revenue and public value.
- Leveraging local sports or event livestreams requires quick coordination between cities, broadcasters, and technology providers.
  
## Solution / Key Development

- Intersection leverages the existing web-capable infrastructure of LinkNYC kiosks to livestream HLS feeds directly from broadcasters, showing the same ads as linear TV.
- Broadcast rights were secured through collaboration with NYC’s Mayor’s Office.
- A selective deployment strategy — only 200 kiosks stream games, while others maintain normal ad rotations.
- Audience targeting uses third-party tools like Geopath for geographical and demographic profiling, focusing on contextual and daypart targeting similar to traditional TV rather than household-level addressability.
- The initiative highlights a hybrid model combining OOH with TV advertising ecosystems in a digital, public context.
