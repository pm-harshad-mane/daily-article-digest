## Summary

Unilever’s Degree brand has been strategically positioning itself ahead of the 2026 FIFA World Cup to maximize its advertising impact through a mix of proactive and reactive media buys. Starting campaigns months in advance, Degree is leveraging official sponsorship, targeted traditional and programmatic media buys, retailer data integration, and social-first strategies to reach and engage relevant audiences. WPP Unite, the agency partner, plays a key role in using first-party, retail, and consumer data to build personalized and effective campaigns that go beyond awareness, aiming to drive brand consideration and direct sales.

## Problem / Workflow Challenge

The core challenge is orchestrating an effective event-based advertising campaign that balances early, proactive media commitments with the flexibility to react to real-time audience behaviors and signals. Degree must also overcome typical issues such as cutting through noise with relevant messaging, integrating retailer and consumer data for dual-sided targeting (retailers and end consumers), and measuring campaign impact beyond traditional awareness metrics to include engagement and conversion in a complex media landscape combining traditional TV, streaming (FAST), programmatic, and social video.

## Solution / Key Development

- Degree secured official FIFA sponsorship and committed to volume buys on traditional event broadcasts months in advance.
- The brand splits spends between fixed event-driven placements (e.g., Telemundo, CBS Sports Golazo Network FAST channel, YouTube creators) and biddable programmatic buys that use advanced data signals.
- Programmatic efforts include Vizio TV home screen takeovers, leveraging Walmart’s retail data to identify relevant households and retail partners.
- Degree’s advertising strategy has evolved to combine traditional demographics with personalized, data-driven messaging across social and video platforms enhanced by social listening.
- Campaign performance measurement focuses on brand lift, engagement, and conversion, moving CTV from pure awareness to a conversion-driving channel through contextual relevance.
- WPP Unite integrates multiple data sources (first-party, consumer behavior, retailer) to build campaigns iteratively and filter signal from noise.

## Why This Matters for My Work

As a Principal Architect at an SSP, understanding Degree’s multi-channel and data-driven World Cup strategy highlights key trends and challenges in programmatic advertising for major events:

- Importance of combining direct media buys with programmatic flexibility to optimize reach and timing.
- Integration of retail data and partnerships to enrich identity resolution and targeting precision, crucial for maximizing monetization opportunities.
- Use of CTV beyond branding into driving conversions with contextual relevance and data-based audience insights.
- Leveraging social listening and first-party data builds the foundation for personalized messaging and more effective campaign activation.
- Campaign measurement tied to multiple KPIs (brand lift, engagement, sales) implicates the need for advanced measurement solutions and potential clean room data collaborations.

These elements reflect growing SSP demands around identity management, cross-channel auction strategies, enriched buyer signals, and performance measurement capabilities in a privacy-conscious environment.
