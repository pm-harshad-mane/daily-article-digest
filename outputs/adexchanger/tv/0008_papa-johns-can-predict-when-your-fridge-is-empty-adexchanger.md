## Summary

Papa Johns partnered with NBCUniversal, Instacart, and Carat to launch a targeted streaming ad campaign that predicts when consumers are low on groceries and most likely to order pizza. By leveraging Instacart’s first-party grocery purchase data, NBCU served tailored ads with QR codes on its streaming platforms (including Peacock and NBC Sports) to audiences identified as having “empty fridges.” The campaign ran from late April through June 2026, aiming to drive order growth, brand affinity, and customer acquisition through precise timing and personalized creatives based on consumers’ food preferences.

## Problem / Workflow Challenge

The key challenge addressed was how to identify and reach consumers at the spontaneous moment of need—specifically when they are low on groceries and more likely to order pizza—without being intrusive or “creepy.” The workflow involved combining behavioral data from grocery purchases with streaming ad targeting to solve the problem of timing ads accurately to moments of high purchase intent, improving ad relevance and effectiveness while maintaining consumer comfort.

## Solution / Key Development

- Instacart’s first-party data, traditionally exclusive to consumer-packaged goods, was made available for the first time to a brand outside that category (Papa Johns) for targeting on NBCU’s streaming inventory.
- NBCU and Instacart created custom audiences based on grocery shopping patterns to predict days when consumers run out of staples.
- Papa Johns delivered dynamically customized ads designed to resonate with different pizza-loving audience segments, including QR codes for immediate ordering.
- Measurement efforts included tracking incremental sales lift from campaign exposure through NBCU and a supporting research study on incrementality.
- The use of QR codes integrated into TV ads marks a step toward more interactive and actionable streamable advertising.
