## Summary

Netflix has enhanced its ad targeting and measurement capabilities since launching its ad-supported platform in 2022 but continues to provide limited data, particularly incomplete or no IP address information, to advertisers and programmatic buyers. This has led to frustration among media buyers and ad tech executives who argue that insufficient IP data hinders precise geotargeting and cross-platform measurement compared to other streaming platforms. Netflix maintains it uses deterministic identity signals instead of IP data and is investing in first-party measurement and conversion APIs. The company has also recruited ad sales and measurement experts from Amazon Ads and partners with data clean room providers like InfoSum, Snowflake, LiveRamp, and AWS to address data transparency concerns. However, some advertisers are reallocating budgets to platforms offering richer data, especially for local targeting and attribution.

## Problem / Workflow Challenge

- Advertisers and programmatic buyers find Netflix's provided data insufficient for sophisticated geotargeting and measurement.
- Netflix offers abridged or no IP address data, reducing geotargeting accuracy significantly (below 50% city-level accuracy).
- Lack of detailed IP-level data complicates reach and frequency controls and cross-platform attribution.
- Netflix's reporting is basic (reach, frequency, completed views) compared to other CTV platforms that provide richer contextual and IP-level data.
- Advertisers fear missing opportunities due to Netflix's data limitations, leading to some reallocation of spend.
- Walled garden data opacity continues as a general industry challenge.

## Solution / Key Development

- Netflix emphasizes deterministic identity signals over IP addresses for privacy and targeting.
- The company has launched a conversion API and is investing in first-party measurement tools, such as brand and conversion lift metrics tailored for its environment.
- Netflix has hired key measurement and ad sales experts formerly from Amazon Ads to strengthen capabilities.
- The streamer works with clean room providers (InfoSum, Snowflake, LiveRamp, AWS) to offer privacy-safe measurement and data collaboration.
- Other major streaming platforms (Paramount, Disney, NBCUniversal) provide more IP data programmatically, posing competition in data availability.
- Netflix currently balances serving big brand advertisers focused on reach while evolving to appeal to digital marketers demanding greater data transparency.

## Why This Matters for My Work

As a Principal Architect at an SSP, understanding Netflix’s evolving ad targeting and measurement approach is critical because Netflix is a major player in streaming inventory with growing programmatic share (50% of its non-live ad sales are programmatic). However, its limited IP address data and reliance on deterministic signals affect how demand-side platforms source data for geotargeting and cross-platform attribution. This presents challenges for integrating Netflix inventory with broader programmatic ecosystems, especially where accurate location targeting and reach/frequency management are needed. The reliance on clean rooms and first-party measurement indicates a market and privacy-driven shift in data access that SSP architectures must accommodate. Aligning with the data transparency demands and tracking needs of advertisers, particularly for local campaigns, will be essential to maintain competitiveness against other CTV providers offering richer data sets.
