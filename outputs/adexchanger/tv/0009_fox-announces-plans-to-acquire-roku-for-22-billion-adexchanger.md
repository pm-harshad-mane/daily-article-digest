## Summary

Fox announced plans to acquire Roku in a $22 billion deal, integrating Roku’s ad tech, first-party data from over 100 million global streaming households, and its popular free streaming service, The Roku Channel, alongside Fox’s own AVOD platform, Tubi. This acquisition significantly expands Fox’s presence and capabilities in connected TV (CTV) advertising, programmatic buying, and data-driven viewer targeting. The combined offering gives Fox a stronger foothold in the competitive CTV streaming and ad tech market, with Roku CEO Anthony Wood remaining with the company post-acquisition.

## Problem / Workflow Challenge

Fox has historically lagged behind competitors like Disney+ and Peacock in building streaming and ad tech capabilities. While Fox owns Tubi, an AVOD service, it lacked the scale and advanced programmatic technology to compete effectively in the rapidly evolving CTV advertising landscape. Fox faced the challenge of enhancing its CTV monetization, programmatic buying, and use of first-party data for targeting and measurement in a highly fragmented streaming market.

## Solution / Key Development

The acquisition of Roku brings Fox a comprehensive suite of ad tech assets, including Roku’s DSP and programmatic buying platform OneView. Fox gains direct access to Roku’s extensive first-party data from over 100 million households worldwide, improving targeting accuracy and measurement capabilities. The combination of The Roku Channel and Tubi creates complementary AVOD ecosystems with minimal audience overlap, expanding Fox’s reach across diverse demographics while keeping the two services separate. The deal positions Fox as a stronger competitor in CTV advertising by leveraging Roku’s scale, data, ad tech infrastructure, and popular streaming properties.

## Why This Matters for My Work

This acquisition signals major consolidation in CTV ad tech, underscoring the importance of owning both streaming inventory and programmatic technology to maximize publisher monetization. For SSP architects, Fox’s enhanced capabilities through Roku’s DSP and first-party data emphasize the growing necessity of deep integrations with DSPs and robust identity solutions to improve auction dynamics, targeting, and measurement on CTV. The combined first-party data sets may also influence privacy and clean room strategies as large players increasingly control access to viewership data. Understanding this deal aids strategic planning around partnerships, data management, identity resolution, and scalable CTV monetization frameworks.
