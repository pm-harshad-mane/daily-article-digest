## Summary

The article covers insights from the 2026 TV upfronts in New York City, where major media companies emphasized "performance" as a key theme in their pitches to media buyers. TV and video publishers are increasingly positioning their inventory as performance marketing channels, aiming to offer more flexibility, control, and measurable results to advertisers. Various companies showcased new tools, dashboards, data partnerships, and programmatic innovations designed to enhance outcomes-based measurement and real-time campaign optimization.

## Problem / Workflow Challenge

Marketers and media buyers are under mounting pressure to prove the effectiveness of their TV ad spend through measurable outcomes. Traditional TV advertising’s legacy of delayed or retrospective measurement complicates real-time optimization and performance-based buying. Additionally, challenges around attribution, particularly for interactive or shoppable TV ads, impede widespread adoption of outcome-based guarantees. Buyers demand faster, more centralized access to actionable data to optimize campaigns mid-flight rather than only after completion.

## Solution / Key Development

- NBCUniversal is rolling out the **Performance Insights Hub** for access to a broad set of outcomes-based measurement providers.
- Fox added new outcomes-based reporting with approximately 20 data vendors integrated into its ad platform.
- Amazon introduced **Dynamic TV Creative**, enabling call-to-action customization based on consumer purchase journey data, leveraging its retail data strength.
- TelevisaUnivision is launching a streaming marketplace and enhancing its household graph for cross-platform audience reach.
- Disney is investing in AI-driven targeting and advertising improvements around premium content, such as sports.
- Warner Bros. Discovery (WBD) unveiled a near real-time **measurement and attribution dashboard** for optimizing campaigns mid-flight and also joined OpenAP’s cross-publisher measurement initiative backed by a conversion API (CAPI).
- Netflix presented advances in ad tech including programmatic pause ads, dynamic ad insertion, and advertiser tools via the Netflix Ads Suite, as well as integration with Amazon and Yahoo DSP.
- YouTube highlighted significant growth in CTV conversions and promoted an approach balancing performance with reach and relevance.
  
Industry collaboration on consolidated impression and outcomes data, alongside conversion APIs, signal a market shift toward more transparent, data-driven, and real-time performance measurement across TV platforms.

## Why This Matters for My Work

For a Principal Architect at an SSP in programmatic advertising, these developments highlight critical areas to prioritize:

- Increasing demand for **outcomes-based measurement** and near real-time campaign analytics necessitates robust integration with multiple data providers and APIs, including conversion APIs (CAPIs).
- The rise of **CTV as a performance channel** means SSPs must support complex targeting, reporting, and dynamic creative capabilities that align TV’s traditionally linear inventory with programmatic performance needs.
- Cross-publisher initiatives and data centralization trends suggest SSPs and publishers will benefit from supporting interoperability standards and open measurement frameworks like those championed by OpenAP.
- Retail media data integration (exemplified by Amazon) points to opportunities for SSPs to partner deeper in identity and deterministic audience signals, balancing privacy with targeted measurement.
- As outcome-based guarantees and shoppable TV formats evolve, SSPs must architect for flexibility in auction workflows and attribution models, anticipating more attribution-based buying models in CTV and video.
- Lastly, real-time dashboards and in-flight optimization capabilities indicate growing need for SSPs to enable fast data flows and actionable insights to publishers and buyers, ensuring monetization agility.
