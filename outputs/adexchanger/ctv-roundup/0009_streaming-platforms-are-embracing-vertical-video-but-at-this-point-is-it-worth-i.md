## Summary

Several major streaming platforms, including Disney+, Netflix, Peacock, and Paramount, are launching or expanding vertical video features on their apps. These features are designed primarily as discovery engines rather than immediate monetization tools, with no current ad opportunities offered. The goal appears to be to compete with TikTok, YouTube Shorts, and Instagram Reels by providing a scrollable feed of short-form, often repackaged content in an ad-free environment to build user habits before introducing ads. Additionally, there is growing industry interest in microdramas—short, vertically-shot episodic narratives—particularly as a potential future content and monetization format. However, the article questions whether these moves by established streamers come too late, as younger users show signs of fatigue toward current short-form content formats.

## Problem / Workflow Challenge

- Streaming platforms aim to compete with social media giants in the short-form vertical video space but face challenges in offering compelling ad-supported formats.
- Establishing user engagement and habit formation without immediate monetization opportunities creates uncertain revenue models.
- The commoditization and mental fatigue associated with current vertical video consumption (e.g., TikTok) challenge sustainable audience retention.
- Traditional entertainment companies may struggle to replicate the financial or cultural success of early microdrama and short-form content creators.
- Brands and advertisers seek innovative ways to integrate marketing within microdramas beyond traditional ad breaks, requiring new workflows for ad placement and measurement.
  
## Solution / Key Development

- Launch of vertical video "feeds" or tabs on major streaming services (Disney+, Netflix, Peacock, Paramount) focusing on content discovery rather than immediate ad revenue.
- Exploration of microdramas as a new vertical video content format that blends episodic storytelling with brand sponsorships and product placements rather than standard ad breaks.
- Emerging direct brand relationships with microdrama platforms, offering new marketing integration methods that could influence future ad tech strategies.
- A strategic pause on ad integration to build an ad-free environment that differentiates streamers from social media and potentially conditions viewers for later monetization.

## Why This Matters for My Work

- SSP and programmatic architects must anticipate how the emerging vertical video and microdrama formats may alter publisher content strategies, requiring adaptation in ad inventory formats and auction dynamics.
- The delayed introduction of ads in vertical video feeds informs how SSPs plan auction inventory and forecast monetization timelines.
- New brand-integrated sponsorship and product placement models within microdramas could reshape programmatic targeting, measurement needs, and creative integrations.
- The evolving landscape of short-form streaming content impacts CTV inventory characteristics, privacy considerations around built-in user habits, and clean room measurement strategies to attribute sponsorship effectiveness.
- Understanding these trends helps SSPs future-proof platform capabilities, data ingestion, and ad execution workflows to support next-generation video formats and brand collaboration models.
