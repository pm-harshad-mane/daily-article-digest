## Summary  
AI startup Rembrand is pioneering programmatic, dynamic insertion of digital billboards within TV content, aiming to create new ad inventory embedded directly into scenes without interrupting the story. The company targets establishing shots or even generates new footage with ad-ready surfaces to offer brands prominent but less intrusive branding opportunities than traditional commercial breaks. Early pilots focus on direct sponsorship deals with full transparency between brands and content owners. Rembrand claims strong advertising performance improvements when combined with traditional TV ads, tapping into a growing preference for less disruptive, in-content ad formats in CTV.

## Problem / Workflow Challenge  
- Traditional product placement and static billboards in filmed content tend to be fixed and non-programmatic, limiting incremental monetization opportunities for publishers.  
- Dynamic ad insertion into scripted content must maintain visual consistency—matching color, tone, and period-appropriate elements—to avoid breaking viewer immersion.  
- Current technology does not enable fully programmatic, large-scale insertion of digital billboards due to complexity in scene matching, legal permissions, and creative control.  
- Creators of original content typically do not have input on post-production ad insertions, raising ethical and creative concerns.  
- Viewer tolerance is variable; subtle in-content ads can be less disruptive but may still provoke backlash if poorly executed or excessive.

## Solution / Key Development  
- Rembrand deploys AI-driven technology to digitally insert ads into TV show backgrounds and establishing shots, even creating new scenes with billboard surfaces for ad placements.  
- Ads are treated roughly as direct sponsorships with transparency, ensuring brands and content owners approve placements.  
- The format is less intrusive, running alongside regular content rather than interrupting it, which improves brand impact metrics such as purchase impact, attention reach, and favorability.  
- This approach aligns with emerging trends in CTV advertising, where new ad formats (overlays, squeezebacks) outperform traditional ad breaks in engagement.

## Why This Matters for My Work  
- SSPs and programmatic architects must consider how dynamic, AI-powered ad insertions in CTV content introduce new auction opportunities and inventory types beyond traditional pre-roll and mid-roll breaks.  
- Maintaining viewer experience while deploying in-content ads requires sophisticated adjustment of creative content and consideration of privacy and measurement implications.  
- As dynamic insertion becomes more programmatic, SSP platforms need integrations that support transparency and brand controls alongside automated inventory delivery.  
- The evolving landscape of less disruptive ad formats demands updates in metrics and reporting within clean rooms to accurately measure impact without traditional impression signals.  
- Ethical and content creator concerns may influence publisher partnerships and inventory policies around in-content ad insertions.
