## Summary

The article discusses how AI is increasingly being used to enhance video advertising content without fully generating it. Advertising experts emphasize AI's role in improving production efficiency and reducing costs, especially for smaller brands entering connected TV (CTV). The focus is on “AI-assisted” content, incorporating AI-driven visual effects, overlays, contextual ad placements, and attention-based targeting to boost viewer engagement. Key industry voices from Google TV, NBCUniversal, Spectrum Reach, and Transmit shared examples of this balanced use of AI, aiming to maintain consumer trust while leveraging AI's advantages.

## Problem / Workflow Challenge

- Consumer skepticism and backlash against fully AI-generated content create a challenge in using AI without alienating audiences.
- Balancing the cost and quality of video ad production, particularly for smaller brands competing on CTV, remains difficult.
- Targeting relevant ads in real-time (e.g., during live sports) to maximize engagement and advertiser ROI is complex.
- Optimizing creatives to resonate with specific audiences and meet key performance indicators (KPIs) requires advanced analytics.

## Solution / Key Development

- Adoption of “AI-assisted” content techniques that enhance human-produced videos with AI-powered visual overlays, camera effects (zooming, panning), and scene-level contextual ad placements.
- Use of AI to improve the timing and contextual relevance of ads, such as dynamically inserting ads during live sports moments to capture viewer attention.
- Leveraging AI for creative auditing and optimization to increase ad effectiveness before campaigns go live.
- These applications provide a middle ground acceptable to consumers who prefer genuine content while enabling publishers and advertisers to drive engagement and performance.
- Brands and publishers utilize AI for operational efficiencies and to create premium-quality, engaging video ads on CTV platforms.

## Why This Matters for My Work

- For an SSP or programmatic architect, understanding AI-assisted content’s role in enhancing CTV ad production and relevance is key to designing platforms that support advanced creative formats and real-time content-aware ad insertions.
- AI-driven contextual targeting and ad timing offer opportunities to improve auction dynamics by increasing engagement and bid value for premium inventory.
- Publishers can leverage AI tools integrated with SSPs to optimize yield and monetize sports and live content effectively.
- Awareness of consumer attitudes toward AI in content should guide implementation of transparency features and creative controls within SSP workflows.
- The use of AI for creative auditing underscores the growing importance of data and measurement capabilities integrated into supply paths to help advertisers optimize campaigns and creatives programmatically.
