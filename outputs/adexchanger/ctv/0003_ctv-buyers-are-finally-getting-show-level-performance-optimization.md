## Summary

CTV advertisers have long struggled to understand exactly which shows their ads run on and how those specific placements impact campaign performance. Typically, streaming platforms provide only limited show-level data post-campaign, often covering just 10-15% of impressions. A new solution involving Peer39, Pontiac Intelligence’s DSP, and InterMedia Advertising uses anonymized content IDs passed through SSP bid requests to deliver near-complete (94%) show-level reporting and performance measurement. This approach allows advertisers to optimize budgets in real time based on granular CPA data per show, improving conversion outcomes significantly. The technology respects user privacy by not revealing household-level data and standardizes content signals that are otherwise inconsistent across publishers.

## Problem / Workflow Challenge

- Streaming platforms traditionally provide limited and delayed show-level ad performance data.
- Self-declared content categories by publishers lack transparency and accuracy.
- Advertisers lack insights into ad effectiveness at the show level during live campaigns.
- Publisher-provided content identifiers are inconsistent and often incomplete.
- Without detailed insights, advertisers can’t efficiently optimize spend by show or content quality, reducing campaign ROI.
- Privacy regulations require anonymization of viewer data.

## Solution / Key Development

- Peer39 ingests anonymized and publisher-provided content IDs for streaming programs, essentially creating impression-level content tracking.
- These IDs travel through SSP bid requests to Pontiac Intelligence’s DSP, which matches ad spend and conversions to specific shows.
- Pontiac embeds tracking pixels on advertiser websites to measure outcomes like purchases, enabling optimization based on CPA signals.
- InterMedia Advertising tested this solution in a CTV campaign for Laundry Sauce, achieving 94% show-level impression reporting.
- Live campaign optimization using show-level CPA data boosted add-to-cart conversions by 64% and improved conversion efficiency by 2.5x.
- The solution adheres to privacy laws (e.g., Video Privacy Protection Act) by avoiding user-level data.
- Other programmatic players like Viant, IRIS.tv, and Beeswax are developing similar content-data integrations.
- This marks a shift toward using show-level data as part of active bidding decisions in open auctions—not just post-campaign reporting.

## Why This Matters for My Work

For an SSP working in programmatic advertising, this development highlights the increasing demand for and feasibility of granular, real-time content-level signals in CTV inventory that can be passed through bid requests. Implementing or supporting standardized, anonymized content ID frameworks can improve transparency and differentiation of streaming inventory quality, enabling more precise buyer optimization and yielding higher yield for publishers. It also underscores the importance of integrating with performance DSPs that can use these signals to optimize campaigns dynamically. Privacy-compliant content-level data sharing can become a new standard for CTV auctions, requiring SSPs to adapt their protocols and enrich bid requests accordingly. This innovation could drive greater programmatic CTV monetization and broaden performance-focused buyer interest in open auction formats.
