## Summary

Digital audio, comprising streaming music and podcasts, captures about 30% of daily media consumption for the average American adult but only commands roughly 3% of total U.S. ad spend. This large mismatch between audience engagement and advertising investment highlights an under-monetized channel. Despite mature programmatic infrastructure and support from major platforms like The Trade Desk, advertisers remain hesitant to increase audio spending significantly. Key challenges include podcast ads often being host-read, which resists programmatic insertion, and streaming audio’s limited integration in agency campaign planning tools. Industry leaders from SiriusXM, Spotify, and The Trade Desk discussed ongoing efforts to embed audio earlier in the media planning process through technology integrations and omnichannel approaches—positioning audio alongside video, display, and CTV in programmatic campaigns. These developments aim to drive greater adoption of automated, data-driven buying for audio, growing budgets, and enhancing measurement and attribution.

## Problem / Workflow Challenge

- Large gap between digital audio consumption (~30% of media time) and ad spend (~3%)
- Podcasts favor host-read ads over programmatic insertion, limiting automation
- Streaming audio is often an afterthought in agency campaign planning, leading to shallow budget allocations
- Audio frequently grouped with terrestrial radio in planning, diminishing recognition of streaming audio’s unique value and data
- Lack of integration of digital audio data into proprietary and omnichannel agency planning tools reduces visibility and prioritization
- Advertisers seek more always-on, guaranteed programmatic deals rather than sporadic sponsorships
- Difficulty ensuring audio is considered alongside CTV, display, and video in omnichannel campaigns

## Solution / Key Development

- Increased programmatic adoption on platforms like The Trade Desk, with audio spend on TTD growing faster than industry average
- SiriusXM’s integration with planning tools like VideoAmp to bring audio into campaign planning earlier
- Major streaming audio platforms (Spotify, SiriusXM, iHeartMedia) fully integrated with all leading DSPs, facilitating programmatic buying
- Use of AI-driven workflow solutions to enhance targeting and automation for audio campaigns
- Audio platforms producing more video content to get included in omnichannel campaign strategies
- Buy-side agencies pushing for audio data upfront in planning cycles to partner with sellers and increase audio allocations
- Growth of programmatic guaranteed and always-on deals around major events (e.g., FIFA World Cup) promoting sustained audio ad investments
- Audio leveraging same identity and measurement frameworks as CTV, online video, and display for unified programmatic buying and performance tracking

## Why This Matters for My Work

For a Principal Architect at an SSP serving the programmatic ecosystem, these insights underscore the continued opportunity and challenges in streaming digital audio monetization through programmatic channels. Key considerations include:

- Enhancing SSP platform support for audio-specific formats and host-read ad inventory alongside standard programmatic placements
- Facilitating greater integration with agency planning and AI-driven tools upstream to drive audio adoption earlier in campaign lifecycles
- Building out identity and measurement frameworks that align audio with CTV, video, and display for true omnichannel programmatic buying
- Supporting programmatic guaranteed and always-on deal types suited to streaming audio’s unique audience and content formats
- Anticipating the growth of video content on audio platforms and enabling cross-format bidding to increase monetization
- Enabling clean room or privacy-compliant data collaboration that respects listener privacy while improving targeting and attribution across audio and connected media
