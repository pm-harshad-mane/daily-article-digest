## Summary

Fox announced its $22 billion acquisition of Roku, expected to close in 2027, positioning Fox as the third-largest player in US television by viewing share. This strategic move merges Fox’s traditional content assets and streaming service Tubi with Roku’s leading connected TV (CTV) platform and subscriber base of over 100 million globally. The deal exemplifies how traditional TV and CTV platforms are converging, focusing on unified audience access, data leverage, and distribution control amid shifting viewer behavior. While Fox still trails dominant streaming platforms like YouTube and Netflix, the acquisition significantly bolsters its CTV presence and advertising capabilities.

## Problem / Workflow Challenge

- Traditional TV companies face declining broadcast viewership as streaming and on-demand content consumption rises.
- Many CTV platforms operate in silos, lacking integration or collaboration that could maximize audience reach and data utilization.
- Advertisers and publishers desire independent, transparent platforms amid concerns about the rise of walled gardens that limit market openness.
- Balancing platform neutrality with owned content and advertising interests poses challenges for conglomerates merging content creation and distribution.

## Solution / Key Development

- Fox’s acquisition of Roku creates a combined content-distribution platform controlling more of the viewer relationship end-to-end.
- Roku’s auto-installation of channels will likely extend to Fox’s Tubi and Fox One, amplifying their reach.
- Access to Roku’s large, data-rich subscriber base enables more targeted advertising and improved viewer experiences.
- The deal strengthens Fox’s leverage in carriage negotiations, creating potential content licensing synergies with other Roku partners.
- The Fox-Roku combination could serve as a blueprint for integrating traditional TV and streaming platforms to meet evolving market demands.

## Why This Matters for My Work

- As an SSP architect, understanding Fox’s acquisition underscores the growing importance of owning and controlling the entire CTV ecosystem—from content through streaming platform to ad inventory.
- The deal highlights how data from large, integrated CTV platforms can empower precise audience targeting and enhance monetization strategies.
- The risks related to walled gardens and platform neutrality signal ongoing challenges in maintaining open RTB and header bidding environments.
- Auto-installed channels on Roku devices demonstrate new distribution methodologies that SSPs must consider when designing auction workflows and integration points.
- The evolving relationship between traditional broadcasters and emerging streaming platforms will affect data privacy compliance, cross-platform measurement, and clean room data collaboration efforts.
