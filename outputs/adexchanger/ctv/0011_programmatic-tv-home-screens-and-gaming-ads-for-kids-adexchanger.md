## Summary

Samsung and Roblox are expanding programmatic advertising into new inventory types and audiences. Samsung is making its smart TV home screen ads programmatic, unlocking premium direct-only inventory to new demand sources. Meanwhile, Roblox, a major gaming platform for children, has introduced ads for users under 13 through a new facial recognition-based age verification system. This allows kids to see untargeted ads while maintaining privacy and compliance safeguards. Both companies emphasize protecting the user ad experience and applying guardrails as they venture into these new programmatic environments.

## Problem / Workflow Challenge

- TV home screen ad inventory has traditionally been sold directly, limiting demand sources and flexibility for SSPs and advertisers.
- Delivering advertising to children in gaming environments raises privacy concerns and regulatory compliance challenges, especially for users under 13.
- Balancing monetization with a non-disruptive user experience on emerging inventory like smart TV interfaces and kid-friendly gaming platforms.
- Implementing age verification and privacy protections without compromising scale or targeting efficacy.

## Solution / Key Development

- Samsung enabling programmatic access to its smart TV home screen ad inventory, broadening demand diversity and auction efficiency.
- Roblox rolling out a facial recognition-based age verification system to confirm under-13 users, allowing ad delivery restricted to untargeted formats, thus maintaining compliance with child privacy regulations.
- Both companies adopting ad experience guardrails to ensure smooth integration of new ads without alienating users.
- These developments highlight innovations in identity verification and privacy-first ad monetization on emerging platforms.
