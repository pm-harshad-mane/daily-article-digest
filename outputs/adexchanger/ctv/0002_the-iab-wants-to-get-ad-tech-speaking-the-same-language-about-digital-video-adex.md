## Summary

The IAB has introduced the Redefining Media Types (RMT) Standard to bring clarity and consistency to terminology around digital video, especially connected TV (CTV). The IAB Tech Lab developed and will maintain the technical specifications. The RMT framework classifies digital video into two layers: media type categories based on consumer viewing experience, and operational attributes concerning technical media buying and measurement. The goal is to reduce confusion across the ecosystem, improve campaign planning and measurement, and prepare for AI-driven media buying. The standard is open for public comment until August 7, 2026, with a finalized version expected later this year.

## Problem / Workflow Challenge

There is a lack of standardized language in ad tech regarding what constitutes CTV and other digital video formats, causing confusion among buyers, sellers, and measurement providers. This ambiguity complicates inventory classification, campaign planning, measurement, and reporting. Additionally, inconsistent definitions hinder interoperability and efficient data exchange, which is increasingly critical as automation and AI-driven media buying grow.

## Solution / Key Development

The IAB’s RMT Standard introduces a two-layer framework to standardize digital video terminology:

- **Media Type Categories:** Organize inventory by how audiences engage with video content (e.g., device type, viewing environment, engagement style).
- **Operational Attributes:** Define technical buying and measurement details such as data signal types, content context, device classification, and addressability.

This approach ensures consumer viewing experience is prioritized while also addressing advertiser and media planner needs. The IAB Tech Lab will maintain the standard, which is designed to foster better interoperability, reduce market fragmentation, and support AI-driven programmatic workflows.

## Why This Matters for My Work

As a Principal Architect at an SSP, consistent definitions of CTV and digital video inventory types are critical to correctly classifying and serving inventory in real-time bidding environments. The RMT framework’s focus on consumer experience combined with operational metadata can improve supply path optimization, targeting accuracy, and measurement fidelity. It also sets a foundation for standardizing data signals that support identity resolution, addressability, and privacy-compliant measurement—key areas in the evolving programmatic ecosystem. Adoption of these standards will help SSPs reduce fragmentation, improve interoperability with DSPs and measurement vendors, and prepare for AI-driven programmatic media buying workflows.
