## Summary
Tatari CRO Andy Schonfeld argues that programmatic is a tool rather than a comprehensive TV media-buying strategy, especially for Connected TV (CTV). He highlights that relying solely on DSPs for programmatic TV buying results in missing significant parts of the TV marketplace, particularly live sports inventory that is commonly sold via direct deals with networks rather than through DSPs. Tatari’s Upstream technology addresses this by enabling automated direct insertion order (IO) buying with major networks, improving measurement via extensive historical spend data, and reducing fees. Schonfeld also emphasizes the role of AI in accelerating media planning and foresees the future of TV media buying hinging on direct access, automation, and convergence.

## Problem / Workflow Challenge
- Overreliance on DSPs to buy CTV inventory misses a large portion of the market, specifically live sports inventory sold directly by networks and not available on DSPs.
- Current programmatic buying workflows do not fully accommodate direct network relationships, leading to gaps in audience reach.
- Holding-company audits have revealed inefficiencies and added fees in the existing programmatic TV supply chain.
- The traditional media-planning process remains time-consuming despite advances in technology.

## Solution / Key Development
- Tatari’s Upstream platform automates direct IO buying with major networks (CBS, Fox, Disney), closing the gap left by DSPs.
- Upstream leverages $8 billion in historical spend data to improve measurement and budget allocation.
- The technology reduces fees typically uncovered during audits by cutting out intermediaries.
- AI is integrating deeply into media planning to compress lengthy planning timelines dramatically.
- Schonfeld advocates for a future TV ecosystem centered on direct deals, automation, and convergence of data and media workflows.

## Why This Matters for My Work
- As a Principal Architect at an SSP, understanding the limits of DSP-only programmatic buying highlights a need to support integrations or partnerships that enable direct deals with networks.
- The push for automation in IO buying and enhanced measurement using historical spend data informs architecture decisions around data storage, analytics, and bidding logic.
- Awareness of fee structures and audit scrutiny suggests designing systems for greater transparency and cost efficiency.
- The rise of AI in media planning may require architecture that supports rapid data processing and dynamic campaign adjustments.
- Future-proofing platforms for convergence between direct and programmatic buying helps SSPs maintain competitive relevance, especially in CTV and live sports inventory.
